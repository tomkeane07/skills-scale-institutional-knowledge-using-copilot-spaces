# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for ensuring usability and a consistent user experience across the product. They translate requirements into user journeys, wireframes, and final UI designs, bridging the gap between customer needs and engineering implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to inform design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Provide design feedback to developers during implementation
- Collaborate with QA to validate accessibility and usability standards
- Maintain and evolve the product's design system

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent user experiences
- Reduce friction and support user task completion
- Ensure design decisions are grounded in user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and handoff sessions with developers
- Usability test results and user research summaries
- Collaboration in sprint planning and backlog grooming with Product Managers

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on requirements and user story definition; translate business needs into user journeys.
- **Developers**: Provide design specs and assets; review implementations for design fidelity.
- **Project Managers**: Align on delivery timelines for design milestones and dependencies.
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate on accessibility and usability acceptance criteria.

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates Agile ceremonies, removes impediments, and continuously supports team improvement. They ensure the team follows agreed Agile practices and help protect focus and delivery flow.

### Responsibilities
- Schedule and facilitate standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that impede team progress
- Support backlog refinement and help maintain a healthy, prioritised backlog
- Coach team members on Agile and Scrum best practices
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope creep during sprints

### Goals
- Improve team velocity and predictability over time
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Ensure Agile ceremonies are productive and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective action items and impediment logs
- Regular coaching conversations with team members and stakeholders

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align on delivery commitments, escalation paths, and sprint-level risks.
- **Product Managers**: Coordinate backlog prioritisation and ensure sprint goals reflect product priorities.
- **Developers**: Support removal of technical blockers and facilitate estimation and planning activities.
- **All roles**: Facilitate ceremonies and promote adherence to Agile practices across the team.

---

## SRE / DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
Site Reliability Engineers (SREs) and DevOps Engineers ensure infrastructure reliability, support release automation, and optimise CI/CD pipelines and incident response processes. They bridge the gap between development and operations to enable safe, frequent, and reliable deployments.

### Responsibilities
- Provision and manage deployment environments (development, staging, production)
- Monitor system health, set up alerting, and lead incident response
- Automate build, test, and release pipelines (CI/CD)
- Define and track Service Level Objectives (SLOs) and error budgets
- Support disaster recovery planning and runbook documentation

### Goals
- Maximise system reliability and minimise mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- Enable rapid, low-risk deployments through automation and tooling
- Reduce manual toil and improve observability across services

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports, post-mortems, and on-call runbooks
- Release readiness reviews and go/no-go discussions
- Infrastructure and capacity planning updates with engineering leads

### Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on deployment processes, environment configuration, and observability instrumentation.
- **Project Managers / Product Managers**: Provide input on release gates, environment readiness, and deployment risk.
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate pre- and post-release verification activities and test environment stability.
- **Scrum Master**: Surface infrastructure-related impediments during ceremonies.
